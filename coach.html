Speaker

Dr. Elisabeth Arnold Weiss is an Associate Professor of Technical Communication Practice. She teaches upper division Advanced Writing and Communication for Engineers and has taught the communication courses for Ph.D. students as well as Freshman Academies. She also teaches in the Aviation Safety and Security Program and has developed the cross-cultural and global community building components of Viterbi’s global innovation course.

After earning her Bachelor’s Degree in Sociology, Dr. Weiss went on to ear her Master’s degree in American Literature and Film for the University of Virginia and Claremont Graduate University. In 2019, she was the recipient of the 2019 Viterbi Dean’s Award for Innovation in Teaching and Education. She has been an Associate Professor at USC for over two decades and has led curriculum in classes within the Engineering school, as well as with the USC School of Dramatic Arts.

Currently, Dr. Weiss serves on multiple boards within USC and is dedicated to providing mentorship for startup teams at USC and the Viterbi entrepreneurial ecosystems that utilizes partnerships with the industry to help develop professional pathways for students. To date, she has initiated two programs, DREAM and “A Board of Trustees for every Student” to provide mentorship at scale across Viterbi.
